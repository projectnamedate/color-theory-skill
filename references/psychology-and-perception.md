## Perception Phenomena

| Phenomenon | Description | Design Impact |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Simultaneous Contrast** | Adjacent colors shift each other's apparent hue (gray on red appears greenish) | Colors never exist in isolation; always test in context |
| **Afterimage** | Staring at a color 30s+ produces its complement on a white surface | Avoid prolonged exposure to intense hues in interfaces |
| **Color Constancy** | Brain compensates for illumination changes; white paper looks "white" under any light | Users perceive "intended" colors across varied screen settings |
| **Bezold Effect** | Color appears to shift toward its surround (fine patterns assimilate) | Small UI elements adopt surrounding hue; test icons/badges in context |
| **Chromatic Adaptation** | Visual system adjusts to ambient color temperature over time | Prolonged exposure to warm/cool UIs shifts user perception |
